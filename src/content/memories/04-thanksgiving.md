Something happened the first time I watched you with my child. The
woman who never tolerated a half finished chore was on the floor
for forty five minutes letting a toddler put stickers on her
sweater. The woman who would not bend on a B was patiently reading
the same picture book for the fourth time, doing all the voices,
laughing at the same page.

I didn't know whether to be touched or to be insulted on behalf of
my eight year old self. *Where was she?* But that was the point.
You were the mother we needed then, and you are the grandmother we
need now, and the line between them is just one life spent loving
us as fiercely as you knew how, in whatever shape we needed.

You always knew the assignment. You still do.
