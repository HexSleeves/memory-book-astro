I brought home a B and you wouldn't sign it. Not yelling, not
angry. Worse than that. *Disappointed.* You sat at the table and
you said the thing you always said: *I know what you're capable of,
and that isn't it.*

I remember being furious. I remember wanting to slam a door. I
remember not slamming it, because doors didn't get slammed in your
house. I remember sitting back down and getting the next test back
to an A, and walking it to the kitchen counter without saying a
word, because I knew you'd already seen it.

You never said *I told you so.* You didn't have to.
